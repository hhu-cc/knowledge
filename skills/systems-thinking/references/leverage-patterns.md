# Common Leverage Patterns

Use these patterns to search for interventions that change system behavior more effectively than local fixes.

## Constraint relief

- Remove or reduce the bottleneck that limits throughput
- Reallocate capacity to the main constraint before optimizing non-bottlenecks

## Incentive redesign

- Align measurement and reward systems with the real outcome
- Check whether local incentives create global damage

## Information flow improvement

- Increase visibility of critical signals earlier in the process
- Reduce latency between event, feedback, and correction

## Policy and rule redesign

- Simplify approval logic that creates unnecessary queues
- Replace blanket rules with threshold-based rules when appropriate

## Queue and handoff reduction

- Reduce unnecessary work-in-progress
- Merge or clarify ownership where delays come from fragmented responsibility

## Behavior-shaping defaults

- Change defaults, templates, or entry points so the desired behavior becomes easier than the undesired one

## Reminder

High leverage is not always high visibility.
A smaller structural change can outperform a large local effort.
