# Problem Intake Template

Use this template to collect the minimum context before reframing a problem.

## 1. Triggering situation

- What is happening now?
- What change, complaint, or signal triggered this request?

## 2. Desired outcome

- What result does success look like?
- What would be different if the problem were solved?

## 3. Observable symptoms

- What can be seen directly?
- What evidence exists today?
- What is a symptom rather than a cause?

## 4. Current assumptions

- What does the team believe is causing the issue?
- Which assumptions have evidence and which do not?

## 5. Constraints

- Time, budget, capability, policy, legal, technical, or organizational limits
- Non-negotiables that narrow the solution space

## 6. Stakeholders

- Who wants the change?
- Who benefits?
- Who bears cost or risk?
- Who can block or approve action?

## 7. Existing metrics

- Which metrics are currently tracked?
- Which are outcome metrics, proxy metrics, or vanity metrics?

## 8. Open questions

- What critical information is missing?
- What evidence would most reduce uncertainty?
