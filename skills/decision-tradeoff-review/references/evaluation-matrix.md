## How to use the matrix

- Compare only real options that could actually be executed.
- Limit the set to a manageable number, usually two to four.
- Use notes to record assumptions, not just scores.
- Highlight disqualifying constraints before detailed scoring.
- Summarize why the winning option wins under the current weights.

## Reminder

A matrix is a thinking aid, not a substitute for ownership.
If the weights change, the recommendation may also change.
